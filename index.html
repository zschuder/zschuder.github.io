About Me

Hello! I'm Zack Schuder, and I'm an undergraduate at the University of California, Berkeley, pursuing a studies in physics and data science. My academic path reflects a deep curiosity about how fundamental science can be used to address real-world challenges—especially in areas like sustainable energy and the management and analysis of large-scale data systems.

At Berkeley, I’ve built a strong foundation in classical mechanics, electromagnetism, optics, and quantum mechanics, and I continue to expand my technical expertise through coursework in computer science and data science, including Python programming, statistics, and machine learning. I’ve also contributed to interdisciplinary projects, such as building a music recommendation system and working with advanced fabrication technologies for scientific applications.

Outside the classroom, I serve as Vice President of the UC Berkeley Men's Club Volleyball Team, where I help lead a competitive, student-run program. In this role, I monitor team finances to ensure sustainable growth, assist in organizing tryouts, travel logistics, and tournament scheduling, and support long-term planning to strengthen the club’s future. This experience has sharpened my organizational and leadership skills, and taught me how to collaborate across diverse teams to accomplish shared goals.

I'm always eager to learn new technologies and take on challenging projects. This portfolio showcases some of my work and highlights my journey in physics. Feel free to explore and get in touch!
