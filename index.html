About Me

Hello! I'm Zack Schuder, and I'm an undergraduate at the University of California, Berkeley, pursuing a studies in physics and data science. My academic path reflects a deep curiosity about how fundamental science can be used to address real-world challenges—especially in areas like sustainable energy and the management and analysis of large-scale data systems.

At Berkeley, I’ve built a strong foundation in classical mechanics, electromagnetism, optics, and quantum mechanics, and I continue to expand my technical expertise through coursework in computer science and data science, including Python programming, statistics, and machine learning. I’ve also contributed to interdisciplinary projects, such as building a music recommendation system and working with advanced fabrication technologies for scientific applications.

Outside the classroom, I serve as Vice President of the UC Berkeley Men's Club Volleyball Team, where I help lead a competitive, student-run program that ranks in the top 5 men's club volleyball teams in the nation. In this role, I monitor team finances to ensure sustainable growth, assist in organizing tryouts, travel logistics, and tournament scheduling, and support long-term planning to strengthen the club’s future. This experience has sharpened my organizational and leadership skills, and taught me how to collaborate across diverse teams to accomplish shared goals.

I'm always eager to learn new technologies and take on challenging projects. This portfolio showcases some of my work and highlights my journey in physics. Feel free to explore and get in touch!

Predicting Housing Prices in Cook County

This project explores what can be learned from an extensive housing dataset that is embedded in a dense social context in Cook County, Illinois. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was used to understand the structure of the data. I then added a few new features to the dataset, while cleaning the data in the process. In this project, I specified and fit a linear model to a few features of the housing data to predict house prices. Error of the model was analyzed with RMSE, and my model was compared with 2,000 peers concurrently working on this project.
